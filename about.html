Our History

MissionNow, Brazil, was inaugurated in 1986. Five years later, Mati and Julie Gali together with Bob and Magda Emberley and some others, began to develop the MissionNow ministry, Projeto Pequinnos (Project Little Ones) which reached out to the little children who on the streets of Recife.

God blessed the ministry and provided a small farm for a base in the nearby town of Camaragibe which later became the MissionNow base.Today Mati and Julie Gali are the leaders of the MissionNow Recife base which has a focus on children, street kids and children at risk.

Mati, born and raised in Samoa, South Pacific joined MissionNow in 1988. Julie was born in Canada and did her DTS in Tyler, Texas, in 1985, before coming to Brazil in 1991. They met and married in Belo Horizonte, Brazil, while working at the Restoration House with MissionNow. They have three children; Jordan, Cory and Sarah.

Over the years, missionaries from various nations and from within Brazil have responded to God’s call to help these lost children. This has resulted in the restoration of hundreds of young lives some of whom are now missionaries in Brazil and other countries. Mati and Julie also travel and minister in China, Haiti, India, England and the South Pacific. Their ministry at home and abroad touches the nations.

Our Vision

Founded in 1960 by Loren and Darlene Cunningham in Lausanne, Switzerland, MissionNow is now present on five continents, with approximately 1,000 bases in about 180 countries (more than 40 in the 10/40 Window). MissionNow has around 18,000 full-time workers who are working to fulfil the commandments of Jesus.

Through short-term mobile teams MissionNow ministers to all the nations of the world mobilizing annually around 30,000 students and volunteers.

MissionNow seeks to awaken as many Christians as possible by involving them in the seven most influential areas of society: Government, Economy, Church, Family, Education, Communication and Celebration.
