Hope House

Hope House or Casa Esperança is our home for children between the ages of 6 and 12, who undergo a family and social reintegration program. Our program basically works in six areas: Spiritual, educational, family, physical, social and professional.

Often having lost even the will to live, Hope House is a place of hope, where the boys can begin to dare to dream once again. They come off the streets having suffered horrific abuse, rejection and trauma. Many, less than ten years old have been involved in a life of prostitution, drug abuse and crime

At first they are unable to either trust or love. They will try to save half their dinner to eat later, unable to comprehend that there will be more food on the table for breakfast in the morning.



Soccer Ministry

Show de Bola How do you reach out to a community that loves to watch and play soccer? Well you start your own soccer club and you make sure it is a winner!

MissionNow Recife is utilising the Brazilian national sport, soccer to reach children and adolescents in the community and teach moral and family values. About 100 students between 8 and 20 years old are enrolled each year in our soccer school. These are divided into 5 categories according to age. There are two days of class each week and one day of discipleship by category.

In addition, we have both family and school participation days, and we compete in some championships in Camaragibe and Recife on the weekends. The Show de Bola project has been running now for 10 years and we rejoice in what God has done in our midst!






Street Team

Street Team The first step in working with street children is to develop a relationship, find out their life history then probe about the possibility of them returning to live with their own family.

If possible visits are then made for the purpose of providing the child’s family with counseling / discipleship and support. In many cases, it is impossible, at least temporarily, for the child to return to family life. We then move them to another phase of the Project.
